# Team Registration

Teams register for each Canestreet edition through the public site. The registration system manages sign-ups, approvals, and team placement into tournament groups.

## For Teams & Players

### How to register

1. Visit the public site during registration period for the current edition
2. Click **"Registra Squadra"** (Register Team)
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Team name:** The official name of your team
   - **Category:** Choose Open, U18, U16, or U14 based on your squad
   - **Player roster:** Add player names (e.g., "Marco", "Luca", "Federica")
4. Submit the form

Your team registration is now **pending** — waiting for the tournament organizers to review.

### After registration

- You'll receive confirmation that your registration was submitted
- Check back on the website or wait for email notification when your registration is **approved**
- Once approved, your team appears in the official roster and will be placed into a group during bracket setup
- If rejected, you'll see the reason and can re-register if the issue is resolved

### Registration statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Waiting for admin review |
| **Approved** | Accepted into the tournament |
| **Rejected** | Not accepted — see the reason and contact organizers if you'd like to reapply |
| **Waitlisted** | Approved pending availability — you'll be contacted if a spot opens |

## For Admins

Navigate to **Admin → Squadre** (Teams) to manage all team registrations.

### Reviewing registrations

The Teams page shows all registrations for the selected edition, organized by status:

- **Pending** teams at the top (require action)
- **Approved** teams (ready for tournament)
- **Waitlisted** teams (backup list)
- **Rejected** teams (not playing)

### Approving or rejecting teams

1. Click on a **pending team** to view details:
   - Team name and category
   - Roster of players
   - Submission date
2. Click **Approve** to accept them into the tournament
3. Or click **Reject** and provide a reason (e.g., "Team name already taken", "Missing roster information")
4. The decision is sent back to the team

### Moving teams to waitlist

If you have more registrations than available spots:

1. Approve your desired team count first
2. For overflow teams, click **Waitlist** instead of approve
3. Waitlisted teams are ranked in the order you mark them
4. If an approved team drops out, you can move the next waitlisted team into an approved slot

### Viewing approved teams

Once a team is approved, they appear in the **Approved** section. This is the roster you'll use when creating tournament groups in the Torneo section.

### Exporting rosters

(If export functionality exists) You can export the approved team roster as a CSV or PDF for printing, sharing with referees, or record-keeping.

## Managing Teams During the Tournament

Once a team is approved, you can still edit their information in the Teams admin if needed:

- Update roster if a player is added/removed
- Correct team name if there was a typo
- Move a team between categories (rare, but possible)

The changes take effect immediately and don't disrupt any groups or matches already scheduled.

## Troubleshooting

**Q: A team submitted a registration but I don't see it.**  
A: Check that you have the correct edition selected (top of the Teams page). Each edition has separate registrations.

**Q: Can I approve a team retroactively if they miss the registration deadline?**  
A: Yes, but you'd need to manually add them. Contact the tournament organizers — they may have a process for late additions.

**Q: What happens if I reject a team by mistake?**  
A: They'll need to re-register. As an admin, you can't "un-reject" — you'd have to approve their new registration once they resubmit.

**Q: Can a team be in two different categories?**  
A: No, each team registration is assigned to one category (Open, U18, U16, U14). If players want to play in multiple divisions, they'd need to form separate teams.

**Q: Why would I use waitlist instead of just rejecting?**  
A: Waitlisted teams are still interested — they're a backup in case approved teams drop out. You can move a waitlisted team to approved without asking them to re-register. It's a courtesy for teams who missed the original cutoff.
