# 3-Point Contest — Shooting Competition

The 3-Point Contest is a separate shooting competition that runs alongside the main tournament. Participants compete in qualifications, semifinals, and finals to determine the best shooter.

## For Spectators

### Watching the contest

On the public site, you can view:

- **Rounds:** The contest is divided into rounds (Qualifications → Semifinals → Finals)
- **Participant scores:** Each round shows all participants and their shooting scores
- **Qualifiers:** Players who advance to the next round are marked with an orange "Qualificato" badge — their row is highlighted in orange
- **Live shooter:** If someone is currently shooting, they're marked with a green pulsing "Live" indicator, so you know who to watch

The page auto-refreshes every 15 seconds, so scores and statuses update without reloading.

## For Admins

### Setting up a contest

Navigate to **Admin → Torneo → 3-Point Contest** tab.

#### Step 1: Create the contest
1. Select the category: **Open** or **Under**
2. Click **"Crea Gara"** (Create Contest) if one doesn't exist yet for that category and edition

#### Step 2: Add players
1. In the **Giocatori** section, type a player's name
2. Click **+** (or press Enter) to add them
3. Players are shared across all rounds — you won't add them per-round

#### Step 3: Create rounds
1. In the **Turni** section, type a round name: e.g., "Qualificazioni", "Semifinale", "Finale"
2. Click **+** to create the round
3. Rounds are numbered automatically in order

#### Step 4: Populate rounds with players
1. Click on a round to expand it
2. Use the **"Aggiungi giocatore"** dropdown to add any contest player to that round
3. Tip: You can add players at any time, even mid-contest, if you miss someone during setup

### Running the contest live

During the actual shooting competition:

#### Enter scores
1. Find the participant in the current round
2. Click the **Punteggio** (Score) field
3. Type their score and press Tab or click away to save

#### Set the live shooter
1. Click the **live indicator** (radio button icon) next to the player currently shooting
2. This broadcasts a green pulsing indicator on the public page so spectators know who's shooting
3. Only one player can be live at a time across the entire contest

#### Mark someone as qualified
1. Check the **"Qualificato"** checkbox next to their name
2. They'll appear with an orange badge on the public page
3. To speed things up, use **"Avanza qualificati"** button to auto-copy all qualified players from the previous round into the current round

#### Reorder players
1. Click the **#** (sort order) field to reorder participants within a round
2. Changes apply immediately

### After the contest

All fields remain editable anytime: scores, qualifications, sort order, even player names. There's nothing locked, so you can always go back and fix something.

## Troubleshooting

**Q: I added a player but they don't show up in rounds.**  
A: You need to add them to each round manually (or use "Avanza qualificati" to copy them from the previous round). Players are stored centrally, but each round maintains its own list of participants.

**Q: Can I delete a player?**  
A: Yes, but this removes them from **all rounds** of that contest. If you only want to remove them from one round, clear their score and uncheck "Qualificato" instead.

**Q: What's the difference between a player and an entry?**  
A: A **player** is a person (e.g., "Marco"). An **entry** is that person's result in a specific round (their score, qualification status, live status). One player can have entries in multiple rounds.

**Q: How do I show the contest on the big screen?**  
A: Check the **Showcase Screen** guide — you can set the showcase to "3-Point Contest - Open" or "3-Point Contest - Under" mode, which displays rounds with auto-scrolling to the live shooter.
